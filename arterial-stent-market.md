<p><p>An arterial stent is a medical device designed to support and maintain the patency of narrowed or obstructed blood vessels, particularly in the treatment of coronary artery disease and peripheral artery disease. These tubular structures are often made from metal or polymer materials and are inserted into arteries during minimally invasive procedures. The primary function of arterial stents is to prevent restenosis, or the re-narrowing of arteries, promoting better blood flow and reducing the risk of cardiovascular events.</p><p>The Arterial Stent Market is witnessing significant growth propelled by factors such as the rising prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, advancements in stent technology, and an increasing geriatric population susceptible to these conditions. Additionally, the shift towards minimally invasive procedures is enhancing the adoption of stenting procedures over traditional surgical interventions. Noteworthy trends include the development of drug-eluting stents, which release medication to reduce the risk of restenosis, and bioresorbable stents, designed to dissolve over time, minimizing long-term complications. The Arterial Stent Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 11.70% during the forecast period, reflecting an increasing demand for effective treatment options in vascular health.</p></p>

<p><strong>The Arterial Stent Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Coronary Stents</li><li>Peripheral Stents</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The arterial stent market is primarily divided into two key types: coronary stents and peripheral stents. Coronary stents are designed to support arteries in the heart, addressing conditions like coronary artery disease by preventing vessel re-narrowing after angioplasty. Peripheral stents, on the other hand, are used in arteries outside the heart, such as those in the legs or renal arteries, to improve blood flow and reduce the risk of complications from peripheral artery disease. Both types are crucial for enhancing vascular health.</p></p>
